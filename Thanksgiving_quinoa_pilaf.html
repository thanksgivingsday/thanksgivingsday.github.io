All you need are three ingredients; fresh or frozen cranberries, sugar, and the zest and juice of fresh oranges. And you can make the cranberry sauce ahead, as far as a week in advance of the meal. (We suggest you make it asap and tick one thing off your Thanksgiving dinner to-do list.) Combine cranberries, sugar, and orange zest in a saucepan; stir to combine. Cover with water and bring to a boil. Cook until cranberries begin to burst, 5 to 10 minutes. Reduce the heat to low, stir in orange juice, and simmer until flavors blend, about 1 hour. Remove from the heat and cool to room temperature, about 20 minutes.
