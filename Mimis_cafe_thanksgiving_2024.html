Labor Thanksgiving Day (勤労感謝の日, Kinrō Kansha no Hi) is an annual public holiday in Japan celebrated on November 23 of each year, [1] unless that day falls on a Sunday, in which case the holiday is moved to Monday. [2] But even though Japan doesn't have Thanksgiving the same way we understand it in the US, Japan still has a Thanksgiving holiday right around the same time. It's called kinrōkanshahi 勤労感謝の日 （ きんろうかんしゃひ ） 勤労感謝の日, or Labor Thanksgiving Day, and it's a national holiday. The modern Japanese Labor Thanksgiving Day became an official holiday in 1948, the year after the installment of the post-war Constitution of Japan. During the time of the American Occupation, the nation reflected on the brutality of the war and a new role for the imperial family was established. Labor Thanksgiving Day is a modern name for the ritual of Harvest Festival (Niinamesai). In the ritual, the Emperor makes the season's first offering of freshly harvested rice to the gods and then eats the rice himself.
